This isn't a how-to guide. It's a mirror.

1 — AI Search Presence
  • My brand shows up when I ask ChatGPT or Perplexity about my product category
  • My brand has its own Wikidata entry with structured data (category, founding year, website)
  • My brand has been described accurately and consistently in at least 3 online sources that AI can reference
  • I know which AI platforms are most relevant to my buyer and have tested all of them
2 — Third-Party Credibility Signals
  • My brand has been covered by at least one major consumer outlet (not trade press)
  • My products appear in "best of" roundup articles that rank in Google search results
  • My brand has verified pages or listings on Influenster, Instacart, or major review platforms
  • My Amazon listings (if applicable) have complete descriptions and category-specific keywords
  • My founder has a byline, feature, or profile in a high-authority publication
3 — Website & Content
  • My website clearly states what category I'm in, who I'm for, and what makes me different — in plain language, not marketing speak
  • My site has an FAQ page that directly answers the questions my customers actually ask
  • My product pages include structured data (schema markup) so AI can parse them
  • I have published content (blog posts, guides, or recipes) that positions my brand as a category authority
4 — Brand Consistency
  • My brand name, category, and key claims are described the same way across my website, Amazon, social, and press mentions
  • My social profiles are complete with consistent bios, location, and website links
  • If someone Googles my brand name, the first page reflects the story I want to tell
5 — Competitive Awareness
  • I've tested how AI describes my top 3 competitors — and I know how their positioning differs from mine
  • I know which specific keywords and phrases trigger AI to recommend brands in my category
  • I have a plan (not just an intention) to improve my AI visibility in the next 90 days

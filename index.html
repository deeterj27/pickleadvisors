Why I Started Pickle Advisors

After 10 years in the startup trenches—from fintech to structured products—I kept coming back to one truth: the most successful companies aren't built on spreadsheets and market analysis alone. They're built on stories, values, and founders who'd rather fail than compromise on their mission.

I've sat across from hundreds of founders, and the ones who succeed aren't always the ones with the best deck or the most polished pitch. They're the ones whose eyes light up when they talk about their product. The ones who can tell you exactly which customer DM made them cry. The ones building something so deeply personal that walking away isn't an option.

This is why I started Pickle Advisors—to partner with founders building at the intersection of physical and digital, where memorable brands meet passionate communities. But more than that, it exists because I believe the best investors don't just write checks—they take the time to understand the human behind the hustle.

Building Trust Through Understanding

When I meet with founders, the first hour isn't about margins or velocity data. It's about understanding you.

What keeps you up at night? What problem did you experience that no one else was solving? What would success actually look like for you—beyond the exit?

This deep understanding allows me to identify growing pains before they happen. When you truly know a founder's values and vision, you can see around corners. You know when they'll struggle with their first co-packer because they're perfectionists. You know when they'll resist raising money because they're bootstrappers at heart. You know when they'll need encouragement to think bigger because they're still amazed anyone wants their product.

Personalized Roadmaps

Every founder's journey is unique. The roadmaps, connections, and guidance stem from understanding your specific vision, not a one-size-fits-all playbook.

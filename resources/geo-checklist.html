Most CPG founders assume their brand is discoverable by AI. Most are wrong. This checklist tells you where you actually stand.

How to use this audit

Answer each question honestly: YES or NO. Check the box for every YES. Don't guess — if you're not sure, the answer is NO.

Your total score at the bottom will tell you exactly how exposed your brand is — and what to do about it.

1
AI Visibility Audit
Do First
  • If a consumer asked ChatGPT for the best brand in your category, would yours appear?
    If not, AI is already sending customers to your competitors — and you may not even know it.
  • Have you tested your brand across multiple AI search tools to benchmark where you stand?
    If you haven't looked, you're flying blind. Your competitors may already be tracking this.
2
Brand Authority Signals
Critical
  • Does your brand exist as a structured entity in the databases that AI models are trained on?
    If not, AI tools have no reliable anchor for who you are — and will either ignore you or get it wrong.
  • Could an AI system accurately describe your brand, category, founding, and differentiators — without visiting your website?
    AI models don't browse the web in real time. If the answer isn't already embedded in their training data, you don't exist.
  • Do you have third-party, authoritative sources that independently confirm your brand is real and legitimate?
    AI tools weigh outside validation far more than anything you say about yourself. Without it, you're just noise.
3
Social Proof & Review Presence
High Priority
  • Are consumers actively reviewing your brand on the platforms that AI tools use as training data?
    Reviews are one of the strongest recommendation signals AI has. Silence reads as irrelevance.
  • Does your presence on major retail and review platforms match the quality of your actual product?
    A great product with thin, outdated, or missing listings tells AI you're not worth recommending.
4
Press & Earned Media
Medium Term
  • Has your brand been covered by publications that carry real editorial authority?
    One article in a trusted outlet can outweigh a thousand social posts in how AI ranks your brand.
  • Could a journalist or AI researcher find enough credible third-party coverage to describe your brand?
    If the only information about your brand comes from you, AI has no reason to trust it — or repeat it.
5
Website & Digital Infrastructure
Foundation
  • Is your website structured in a way that AI crawlers can understand what your brand is and what it sells?
    A beautiful website means nothing if machines can't parse it. AI needs structure, not just design.
  • Does your digital footprint tell a consistent, authoritative story — or is it scattered and contradictory?
    Inconsistent information across the web confuses AI models. Confused models don't recommend.

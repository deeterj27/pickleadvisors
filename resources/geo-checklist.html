What is GEO?

Generative Engine Optimization is the practice of optimizing your brand's digital presence so AI tools — ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Google AI Overviews — recommend you when consumers ask for products in your category.

Unlike traditional SEO, GEO is about leaving the right signals in the right places so LLMs can find, understand, and recommend your brand. The brands doing this today are building a moat that won't be easy to replicate in 12 months.

1
Audit Your Starting Point
Do First
  • Test your brand in 3 AI tools
    Ask ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini: "What are the best [your category] brands?" Screenshot results as your baseline.
    15 min
  • Search your brand on Google
    AI models are trained on Google-indexed content. Count pages: website, press, reviews, retailer pages, social profiles.
    10 min
  • Check for a Wikidata entry
    Search wikidata.org — one of the highest-trust signals for how LLMs form brand knowledge.
    5 min
🧠
How LLMs decide what to recommend
LLMs don't browse the internet live — they're trained on web snapshots. Wikipedia/Wikidata, review aggregators, and high-authority press carry disproportionate weight. One article in a trusted outlet can outperform 100 social posts.
2
Build Your Wikidata Entry
Highest Impact
  • Create a Wikidata account
    Free at wikidata.org. Takes 2 minutes.
    2 min
  • Create a new item for your brand
    Add brand name + short description (e.g., "American CPG brand specializing in high-protein snacks").
    10 min
  • Add structured statements
    Key: instance of, industry, country, official website, founded, HQ location, product category.
    20 min
  • Link official website + Wikipedia
    Under "Identifiers" add your website. Link Wikipedia if applicable — Wikidata alone still sends strong signals.
    5 min
3
Get Indexed on Review Aggregators
High Priority
  • Influenster — claim your brand page
    Major consumer review platform AI tools heavily reference. Encourage customers to leave reviews.
    30 min
  • Amazon — complete, keyword-rich listings
    Amazon pages are heavily indexed by AI. Full title, description, bullet points, and category tags increase discoverability.
    45 min
  • Google Shopping — active product feed
    Product titles should include category keywords. Feeds directly into AI shopping recommendations.
    30 min
  • Instacart — optimize brand page
    Increasingly referenced by AI for grocery and food brand recommendations. Complete product descriptions.
    20 min
4
Get Press — The Right Kind
Medium Term
  • Prioritize consumer outlets over trade press
    Target: Well+Good, Eat This Not That, Men's/Women's Health, Healthline, Delish, Food52 — high in LLM training data.
    Ongoing
  • Submit to "best of" roundup articles
    "Best protein bars," "healthiest energy drinks" — these listicles carry enormous weight with AI. Use HARO and Qwoted.
    Ongoing
  • Get a Forbes / Inc. / Entrepreneur feature
    High-trust outlets for LLMs. Founder features become permanent AI knowledge signals. Consider contributor networks.
    2–4 wk
5
Optimize Your Website for AI
Technical
  • Add Schema.org structured data
    Add Product, Organization, and BreadcrumbList schemas. On Shopify: Schema Plus or Yoast.
    1–2 hr
  • Write a clear, keyword-rich About page
    State: what the product is, who it's for, what problem it solves, what makes it different. Use natural language.
    45 min
  • Create an FAQ page for your category
    "Is [product] healthy?" "How does it compare to [competitor]?" Clear answers become AI reference material.
    1 hr

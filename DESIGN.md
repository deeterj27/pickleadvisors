## Overview

Pickle Advisors should feel like the premium operating partner for consumer brand founders whose growth is starting to outrun their systems. The brand is commercially direct, founder-aware, and operator-led. It should not feel like a generic SaaS landing page, a playful food brand, or a soft agency site.

The site should communicate one core promise: Pickle turns messy reporting, pricing, inventory, cash, and workflow problems into a cleaner operating layer that founders and teams can actually use.

## Colors

- **Primary / Ink (#141414):** The core advisory color. Use for headlines, dark sections, footer, and serious conversion moments.
- **Secondary / Muted Stone (#655F56):** Body copy and supporting metadata. Keep contrast strong enough for mobile readability.
- **Tertiary / Pickle Green (#0E7A3F):** Signature brand accent. Use for logo marks, selective labels, proof details, and restrained highlights.
- **Accent / Advisory Red (#E8112D):** Primary conversion color for CTAs and urgent commercial cues. Use sparingly so it keeps authority.
- **Neutral / Warm Cream (#FFF8F0):** Default page canvas. Adds consumer-brand warmth and keeps the advisory from feeling too cold or technical.
- **Neutral Deep (#F5EBDD):** Alternating section background for engagement and proof modules.
- **Border (#E5D8C8):** Warm hairline rule for cards, rows, and editorial separators.

## Typography

Use **Instrument Serif** for major headlines to move the brand from utilitarian AI site toward premium editorial advisory. Use **Inter** for body, labels, buttons, nav, and data-heavy modules.

Headlines should be large, confident, and slightly tight. The serif can be italicized sparingly for key emphasis words, never for full paragraphs. Body copy should be compact and commercially direct. Labels should be uppercase but used selectively; too many all-caps labels make the site feel mechanical.

## Layout

Prefer editorial asymmetry over repetitive card grids. Use sticky intros, row-based proof lists, and three-card engagement modules only when they clarify the buying path.

Core section order:

1. Pain-led hero with one primary CTA and one lower-friction audit path.
2. What gets installed.
3. Who this is for.
4. How the engagement works.
5. Why Pickle is credible.
6. Founder proof.
7. Media/network as supporting advantage.
8. Final CTA.

Desktop should feel spacious and premium. Mobile should prioritize fast comprehension: clear headline, full-width CTAs, compressed cards, no horizontal overflow, and readable body copy at 390px and 430px.

## Elevation & Depth

Use subtle depth only. Prefer borders, warm surfaces, and soft gradients over heavy box shadows. Dark hero modules can use deeper shadow because they sit inside a high-contrast environment. Light cards should feel like printed editorial panels, not SaaS dashboard widgets.

## Shapes

Buttons are pill-shaped. Cards use 22-28px radius. Fine separators and row borders should be warm, light, and precise.

## Components

- **Primary CTA:** Pickle green background, white text, pill radius. Use for booking the strategy call. Red is reserved for small urgency accents, not the default action language.
- **Secondary CTA:** Transparent or white surface with green/ink text. Use for the AI audit path.
- **Proof chip:** Low-contrast dark card in the hero with a bold proof point and short label.
- **Fit row:** Editorial row with a short buyer-state label and one explanatory paragraph.
- **Engagement card:** Three-step commercial model: audit, buildout, operating partner.
- **Media card:** Dark surface, restrained green label, framed as market intelligence and founder network, not a separate entertainment business.

## Do's and Don'ts

**Do**

- Make revenue, margin, cash, inventory, and founder time visible.
- Show concrete deliverables: dashboards, pricing models, forecasting views, SOPs, automations, alerts, reporting cadence.
- Keep AI positioned as part of the operating system, not a vague magic promise.
- Use warm editorial spacing and a premium founder-advisory tone.
- Preserve the consumer-brand wedge while keeping the offer broad enough for retail, ecommerce, wellness, beauty, lifestyle, and CPG.

**Don't**

- Do not overuse neon green or make the site feel like a generic AI tooling startup.
- Do not use emoji-style icons, playful clip art, or rainbow accents.
- Do not let Deets Eats media links distract from the advisory conversion path.
- Do not publish DIY audit instructions that let prospects solve the problem without booking.
- Do not make button text shouty or overly mechanical when a calmer premium CTA works better.
